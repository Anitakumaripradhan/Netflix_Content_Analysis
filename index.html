Introduction

Netflix, a popular OTT platform, is analyzed here to understand the distribution of content available from a dataset sourced from Kaggle. This project focuses on analyzing content by type, rating, and country. It covers overall data cleaning, visualization, and an in-depth analysis of content distribution across countries, age groups, and content types.

Data Cleaning and Formation

  • There were 2634 director, 825 cast, 831 country, 10 date added, 4 rating, and 3 duration values missing.
  • All missing values were dropped using pandas.
  • Duplicate rows were identified and removed.
  • The 'date added' column was formatted into datetime format.
  • Years were extracted from the 'date added' column and stored in a new column 'year added'.

Movies Across Countries

This graph illustrates that the United States contributed the highest number of contents, with India ranking second, followed by the UK, Japan, South Korea, and Canada. The significant drop after the US indicates Netflix's strong presence in Hollywood. The further drop after India suggests an ongoing expansion effort in the Indian OTT market, which holds immense potential, especially after the COVID-19 pandemic.

Netflix Show Type Distribution

This graph demonstrates that the number of movies is more than double the number of TV shows on Netflix. This trend likely reflects user preferences, where there's a higher demand for movies, and Netflix's content strategy aligns with this demand-supply dynamic.

Rating Wise Distribution

In this graph, TV-MA ratings account for over 3000 content pieces, and TV-14 ratings have approximately 2000. Following these, the number of content drastically decreases. This distribution suggests that Netflix's content caters predominantly to users aged 14 and above.

Content Over Time

This graph shows a significant increase in Netflix content after 2016, coinciding with Netflix's expansion into over 190 countries with multi-language content. The number of OTT users drastically increased after 2019 due to the COVID-19 pandemic, leading to theater closures and direct movie releases on OTT platforms. Increased competition post-2019 also prompted Netflix to heavily invest in more content to attract and retain users.
